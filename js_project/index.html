Animals adapting through a change in color is not a new concept. The chameleon is famous for its camoflauge tactics. Another animal known for changing colors is the Golden Tortoise Beetle. The Golden Tortoise Beetle changes from a beautiful golden color to a scarlet color whenever threatened by a predator. This red hue makes predators think the beetle is not safe to eat, leaving the beetle to live another day.

Predators

Changing colors in this way protects the Golden Tortoise Beetle from predators.

The Golden Tortoise Beetle has two parasitoids as well as other predators. The wasp parasitoid Tetrastichus cassidus Burks and the fly parasitoid Eucelatoriopis dimmocki live in close association with the beetle and benefit from this association at the beetle's expense. Other predators of the Golden Tortoise Beetle inclue ladybird beetles, damsel bugs, sheild bugs, and assassin bugs.
